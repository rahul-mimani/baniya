BillMaker · self-hosted capacity

How far one free deployment goes — mobile users, admins, and clients.

BillMaker runs on the free tiers of Firebase, Supabase, Cloudflare Workers, and Brevo. The numbers below are for a single deployment — one Firebase project, one Supabase project, one Worker — which is multi-tenant and hosts many shops at once. They're planning estimates: the real ceiling depends on how many bills you write and pages you open per day.

The short answer

free tier · per deployment
Mobile devices ~200 online at once Shop-staff phones/tablets creating bills, live-syncing together. bound by Supabase Realtime — 200 concurrent connections free.
Admins / shop No limit 1–10 typical Portal accounts that manage catalog, customers & bills. headroom no code cap — add any number via the portal.
Clients served 1,000s of accounts Customers who log into the client portal to see their bills. bound by ~300 logins/day (OTP email) & storage.

A typical small shop uses 2–5 staff devices, 1–3 admins, and anywhere from a handful to a few thousand customers — so a single free deployment comfortably covers roughly 10–50 small shops before any limit bites.

Why it fits small-to-medium shops

the sweet spot

BillMaker is deliberately built local-first on free infrastructure — a design that trades limitless scale for zero cost, offline resilience, and near-zero ops. That's exactly what a single shop or a small chain needs, and exactly where a big-box enterprise stack would be overkill.

Built for this ideal

  • Corner shops & small chains — 1 to ~50 outlets, a few billing devices each.
  • Patchy internet — the app is offline-first: it works with no signal and syncs when it's back, so a dropped connection never blocks a sale.
  • Tight budgets — the whole stack runs on free tiers; you pay only after real growth.
  • No ops team — no servers to run; managed Firebase/Supabase/Cloudflare do the heavy lifting.
  • Hundreds–thousands of bills/day and up to a few thousand customers on the client portal.

Reach for more when outgrown

  • Hundreds of POS lanes billing at once — past the ~200 live-connection comfort zone.
  • Millions of transactions/day — beyond what shared free-tier write budgets absorb.
  • Sub-second, cross-region consistency or heavy analytics/warehousing needs.
  • Strict enterprise compliance (dedicated tenancy, audit pipelines, SSO).

At that scale you'd lift the paid tiers below — and eventually graduate to dedicated infrastructure.

Per shop vs. per deployment

multi-tenant

One shop shops/<code>

All of a shop's data lives under its own shop_code. A shop is a few staff devices, a few admins, and its own customers. Nothing in the code caps devices, admins, or clients per shop — the shared cloud limits are what you divide up.

One deployment many shops

A single Firebase + Supabase + Worker hosts many shops at once, each isolated by shop_code. The free-tier limits above are shared across all of them — so growth is about total bills and logins per day, not shop count.

Scaling past the free tiers

pay only where you hit a wall

Each limit lifts independently — you upgrade only the one service you've outgrown, and the app needs no code changes.

pay-as-you-go
Firebase Blaze removes the 20k-write / 50k-read daily caps — you pay pennies per extra 100k operations. This is the main lever for more bills and more shops.
~$25 / mo
Supabase Pro → 8 GB database, ~500 concurrent realtime connections, higher bandwidth, and no auto-pause. Lifts the ~200-device ceiling and the storage cap.
~$5 / mo
Cloudflare Workers Paid → 10 million requests/month included, for high client-portal traffic.
paid plan
Brevo (or another email provider) → thousands of emails/day, so many more sign-ins per day.
free
Split the load — give a busy shop its own Worker (WORKER_PROFILE=secondary) so cron sync spreads out. No extra cost.

Assumptions & caveats

  • Estimates, not guarantees. Real ceilings depend on bills written and pages viewed per day — a read-heavy admin dashboard on a large catalog spends the Firestore read budget faster.
  • Auth isn't Supabase Auth. BillMaker uses its own OTP + JWT, so client accounts are ordinary rows — there's no 50k monthly-active-user cap; storage and email throughput are the real limits.
  • Mobile is offline-first. Devices work with no connection and sync when back online, so brief connection spikes don't break anything — the 200-connection number is about simultaneously live devices.
  • Supabase free projects pause after ~7 days of inactivity — fine for a live shop, worth knowing for a demo.
